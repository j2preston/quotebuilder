# QuoteBot — Outreach & Messaging Guide

---

## The core message

**Problem:** Tradespeople lose jobs because quotes take too long.  
**Solution:** Describe the job out loud. Quote sent to the customer in 60 seconds.  
**Proof:** Works from your phone. Uses your rates. Sends on WhatsApp.

---

Pick one depending on where you're using it:

| Situation | Headline |
|---|---|

## In-person pitch

**Step 1 — open with their pain:**

> "You ever finish a job in the morning, tell yourself you'll send the quote later, 
> then it's 9pm and you still haven't done it? And by then the customer's already 
> gone with someone else?"

Wait for the nod. Every tradesperson has felt this.

**Step 2 — one sentence solution:**

> "QuoteBot fixes that. You just describe the job into your phone while you're 
> still at the property, and it sends a proper quote to the customer on WhatsApp 
> before you've driven away."

**Step 3 — shut up.**

Let them ask questions. The questions will tell you exactly what matters to them.

---

## Common questions & answers

**"How does it know my prices?"**
> You set your labour rate and call-out fee when you sign up. Every quote uses your 
> numbers — it never guesses.

**"What does the quote look like?"**
> A proper PDF — your name, their name, itemised breakdown, VAT if you're registered, 
> deposit amount. Looks like something you'd spend an hour doing in Word.

**"Does the customer need an app?"**
> No. They just get a WhatsApp message with a PDF attached. Nothing to download.

**"What if I do a job that's not standard?"**
> You can edit any quote before sending — add lines, change prices, adjust quantities. 
> It's a starting point, not a cage.

**"How much does it cost?"**
> [Insert your pricing here]

**"What trades does it work for?"**
> Right now it's set up for electricians, with more trades coming. If you're an 
> electrician, it's ready to go today.

## Demo script (showing someone the app)

Walk through this in under 2 minutes:

1. **Open the app** → "This is the dashboard — you can see your quotes here"
2. **Tap Dictate** → "This is how you create a quote"
3. **Hold the button, say:** *"Replace consumer unit at a 3-bed semi, customer is 
   Mrs Davies in Manchester, standard urgency"*
4. **Release** → let it process → "It's extracting the job details and pricing it 
   up using my rates"
5. **Quote appears** → "There it is — itemised, VAT calculated, deposit worked out"
6. **Tap Send** → enter a WhatsApp number → "And that's the customer getting it now"
7. **Close with:** *"That's 60 seconds. How long does it take you at the moment?"*

## What NOT to say

- ~~"AI-powered"~~ → say "it works out the price automatically"
- ~~"Platform"~~ → say "app" or "tool"
- ~~"SaaS"~~ → never
- ~~"Machine learning"~~ → irrelevant to them
- ~~"Scalable solution"~~ → they will walk away
- ~~Monthly pricing upfront~~ → get them interested first

## LinkedIn

Tradespeople aren't on LinkedIn — but the people around them are.

**Who to target on LinkedIn:**
- Trade association reps (NICEIC, NAPIT, ECA, Gas Safe)
- Merchant account managers (Rexel, Edmundson, City Electrical Factors)
- Trade press journalists (Electrical Times, The Electrician magazine)
- Accountants and business coaches who work with trades
- Investors / founders if you're thinking about funding

**Tone on LinkedIn:** More founder story, less sales pitch.

## Early user ask

When someone tries it, follow up personally:

> *"How did the first quote go? Anything feel off or missing?"*

Early feedback is more valuable than early revenue. Get 10 tradespeople using it 
daily before worrying about anything else.
